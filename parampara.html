Guru Parampara

The unbroken lineage of Acharyas preserving Madhwa Siddhanta

The Mutt honours the following Acharyas in succession. The list below is presented with reverence. The current Peetadhipati is highlighted. Our lineage follows the Madhwa Siddhanta tradition, tracing back to Sri Madhvacharya, the founder of Dvaita Vedanta.

The Madhwa Lineage

The tradition begins with Sri Madhvacharya (1238-1317 CE), who established Dvaita Vedanta. His philosophy emphasizes the eternal distinction between the Supreme Lord Vishnu and individual souls, and the reality of the material world. The subsequent Acharyas have preserved and propagated these teachings through an unbroken Guru-Shishya parampara.

Balagaru Akshobhya Tirtha Mutt was first headed by Sri Lokavandita Teertharu (1374), the 6th in the parampara. The mutt is located on the banks of river Tunga in Baalagaru village, near Tirthahalli. This mutt is also the Moola Mutt (root mutt) of Kangu Mutt (Kangu means Adike).
